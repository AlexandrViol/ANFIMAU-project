Key steps of the business analysis process

Our business analysts have broad experience in working with organizations to identify areas where they can improve accuracy, performance, and efficiency. This helped us work out an effective approach to achieving the maximum value from the services provided.

1
1

Initial requirements elicitation

Our experts start by gathering all the details related to the project. This information includes the domain, factors that can potentially affect the project strategy, the history of the project, and its risks and constraints.

2
2

Research & analysis

At this stage, we carry out meticulous research of the client’s business, its niche, competitors, current market trends, and demand, as well as uncover issues and find their root causes. In brief, we examine all the internal and external circumstances affecting the client’s business.

3
3

Primary business objectives

Before jumping into defining requirements, we agree on the business needs early with the parties involved. Thus, our experts discover expectations from your primary stakeholders, reconcile conflicting expectations, and ensure the business objectives are clear and actionable.

4
4

Scope definition

Our business analyst works closely with the project manager to define what is to be achieved by the project. As a result, you get a clear scope statement that provides your project team with the go-forward strategy and helps participants envision their contribution to the project.

5
5

Business analysis plan

Within this stage, our experts provide a detailed timeline for delivering the requirements to the development team. The timeline is determined by such factors as the project scope and methodology, as well as stakeholders’ availability.

6
6

Business requirements presentation

Our business analyst clarifies functional and non-functional requirements and gets the approval to deliver them further to the development team. To maximize efficiency, we sequence your deliverables to facilitate development plans and avoid complexities.
